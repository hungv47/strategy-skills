# Customer Interview & Research Methodology

Complements public VoC collection (Reddit, G2, forums) with direct interview methodology, support ticket analysis, and win/loss insights.

---

## When to Use Direct Research

Public VoC (Step 2 in SKILL.md) reveals what people say openly. Direct research reveals what they won't post publicly:

| Method | Reveals | When to Use |
|--------|---------|------------|
| **Public VoC** | Open complaints, praise, comparisons | Always — it's the starting point |
| **Win/loss interviews** | Why they chose you (or didn't) | When conversion is the focus |
| **Customer interviews** | Deep motivations, hidden pains, workflow details | When personas need depth |
| **Support ticket analysis** | Friction points, feature gaps, emotional intensity | When retention is the focus |
| **Sales call analysis** | Objections, decision criteria, buying process | When the sales process is the focus |

## Win/Loss Interview Framework

### Who to Interview

| Type | Find Them Via | What They Reveal |
|------|-------------|-----------------|
| **Recent wins** (signed in last 30 days) | CRM, signup data | What tipped them to buy |
| **Recent losses** (evaluated but didn't buy) | CRM, abandoned trials | What stopped them |
| **Churned** (cancelled in last 60 days) | Billing system | What failed for them |
| **Competitor users** | Communities, LinkedIn | What they chose instead and why |

### Interview Structure (20-30 minutes)

**Opening (2 min):**
"I'm researching how people in [role] evaluate [category] tools. This isn't a sales call — I'm trying to understand your experience honestly."

**Context (5 min):**
1. "What triggered you to start looking for a [category] solution?"
2. "What were you doing before — what was your workaround?"
3. "Who else was involved in the decision?"

**Evaluation (10 min):**
4. "What alternatives did you consider? How did you find them?"
5. "What were your must-have criteria? What were nice-to-haves?"
6. "What almost stopped you from choosing [product / alternative]?"

**Decision (5 min):**
7. **For wins:** "What was the moment you decided to go with us?"
8. **For losses:** "What was the deciding factor in going with [competitor]?"
9. "If you could change one thing about [product], what would it be?"

**Close (3 min):**
10. "Is there anything I should have asked but didn't?"
11. "Can I follow up if I have a quick question later?"

### What to Listen For

| Signal | What It Means |
|--------|--------------|
| Emotional language | This is a real pain, not a hypothetical one |
| Specific competitor names | Active evaluation, not casual browsing |
| Workaround descriptions | They've already invested effort — switching cost matters |
| "I wish..." statements | Feature gaps or positioning opportunities |
| Silence or hedging | Sensitive topic — the real insight is nearby |
| Unprompted comparisons | What they actually care about (not what you ask about) |

## Support Ticket Analysis

### Mining Support Tickets for ICP Insights

Support tickets are unfiltered VoC — customers write them when something matters enough to complain.

**Analysis workflow:**
1. Pull last 90 days of tickets
2. Categorize by topic (feature request, bug, confusion, billing, onboarding)
3. Rate emotional intensity (1-5) based on language
4. Identify patterns: which issues cluster together? Which personas file which tickets?

### What to Extract

| Category | ICP Insight |
|----------|------------|
| **Repeated confusion** | Onboarding gap — your mental model doesn't match theirs |
| **Feature requests** | Unmet needs — potential positioning angles |
| **Angry tickets** | High-pain moments — use the language in marketing (shows you understand) |
| **"Cancel because..." tickets** | Churn drivers — feed to `problem-analysis` when diagnosing retention issues |
| **Praise / thank you tickets** | Success moments — testimonial candidates |

### Emotional Intensity Scoring

| Score | Language Signals | Insight |
|-------|-----------------|---------|
| 1 | "Just wondering," "Quick question" | Low pain, informational |
| 2 | "It would be nice if," "I'd prefer" | Mild frustration, feature want |
| 3 | "This is confusing," "I can't figure out" | Real friction, potential churn risk |
| 4 | "This is broken," "I'm frustrated" | High pain, needs immediate response |
| 5 | "I'm considering cancelling," "This is unacceptable" | Critical — save intervention needed |

Scores 4-5 are your richest VoC source. Pull exact quotes — this is how your audience describes pain when they're not filtering for a public audience.

## Synthesis: Combining Sources

When combining public VoC with direct research, weight findings by evidence strength:

| Source | Evidence Strength | Why |
|--------|------------------|-----|
| Win/loss interviews (10+) | Highest | Direct, recent, decision-focused |
| Support tickets (pattern across 20+) | High | Unfiltered, repeated, specific |
| Customer interviews (5+) | High | Deep, contextual, nuanced |
| Public VoC (G2, Reddit, 15+ quotes) | Medium-high | Real but filtered for public audience |
| Sales call notes | Medium | Filtered through sales rep interpretation |
| Survey responses | Medium | Structured but potentially satisficing |
| Assumptions without evidence | None | Don't use these |

**Rule:** If interview data contradicts public VoC, the interview data usually wins — people are more honest in private than in public forums.
