# Voice of Customer Analysis Guide

Capture and analyze authentic customer language to inform messaging, positioning, and product decisions.

---

## Quote Collection Framework

### Minimum Quote Requirements

Collect at least 15 direct quotes across these categories:

| Category | Minimum Quotes | Purpose |
|----------|----------------|---------|
| Pain Expression | 5 | Understand frustration language |
| Solution Seeking | 3 | See what they've tried |
| Success/Hope | 3 | Identify what resonates |
| Decision Criteria | 2 | Understand evaluation process |
| Budget/Price | 2 | Price sensitivity signals |

### Quote Documentation Template

For each quote captured, record:

```
Quote: "[Exact wording from source]"
Platform: [Reddit/Twitter/G2/etc.]
Context: [Thread title, discussion topic, trigger]
Emotional Tone: [Frustrated/Desperate/Hopeful/Skeptical/Excited]
Pain Level: [1-10]
Relevance: [Which product/feature this relates to]
Date: [When posted]
```

## Quote Categories & Examples

### Pain Expression Quotes

Capture how they describe frustrations:

**Format:**
```
"[Exact quote about their frustration]"
[Platform - Community/Thread - Context]
Emotional Tone: [Frustrated/Desperate/Hopeful]
```

**Example Quotes to Find:**
- "I've been struggling with X for months..."
- "Why is it so hard to just..."
- "Every time I try to X, Y happens..."
- "I'm so tired of dealing with..."
- "This is driving me crazy..."

**What to Look For:**
- Intensity words (always, never, every time, so frustrating)
- Time indicators (for months, every day, constantly)
- Comparisons to expectations (should be simple, why can't I just)

### Solution Seeking Quotes

Capture what they've tried:

**Example Quotes to Find:**
- "I tried [solution] but..."
- "Has anyone found a way to..."
- "I've been using [tool] but it doesn't..."
- "Looking for recommendations for..."
- "Currently doing [workaround] but it's not ideal..."

**What to Look For:**
- Named competitors and alternatives
- DIY solutions and workarounds
- Feature requests hidden in complaints
- Reasons for rejection ("but it's too expensive", "but it's too complicated")

### Success/Hope Quotes

Capture what worked or gave hope:

**Example Quotes to Find:**
- "Finally found something that works..."
- "This changed everything for me..."
- "After trying X, I discovered..."
- "The thing that made the difference was..."
- "Wish I had known about this sooner..."

**What to Look For:**
- Specific features or benefits praised
- Transformation language (before/after)
- Recommendation patterns
- What tipped them from skeptical to believer

### Decision Criteria Quotes

Capture how they evaluate options:

**Example Quotes to Find:**
- "The most important thing for me is..."
- "I chose X over Y because..."
- "What convinced me was..."
- "I need something that..."
- "Deal breaker for me is..."

**What to Look For:**
- Priority rankings
- Must-have vs. nice-to-have features
- Trust signals they mention
- Comparison frameworks they use

### Budget/Price Quotes

Capture price sensitivity:

**Example Quotes to Find:**
- "I'd pay X for something that..."
- "Too expensive for what you get..."
- "Worth every penny because..."
- "Looking for something more affordable..."
- "The ROI just isn't there..."

**What to Look For:**
- Price anchors they mention
- Value perception language
- ROI expectations
- Budget constraints and approval processes

### Metaphors & Analogies

Capture how they describe problems and solutions:

| Metaphor/Analogy | What It Describes | Insight |
|------------------|-------------------|---------|
| "Like herding cats" | Managing X | Chaos/lack of control |
| "Needle in a haystack" | Finding Y | Difficulty/frustration |

### Urgency Indicators

Words/phrases that show priority level:

**High Urgency:**
- "I need this yesterday"
- "Burning platform"
- "Can't wait any longer"
- "Desperate for a solution"

**Medium Urgency:**
- "Would be nice to have"
- "Looking to improve"
- "Exploring options"

**Low Urgency:**
- "Someday we should"
- "Not a priority right now"
- "Nice to have"

### Authority Signals

How they establish credibility:

- "In my X years of experience..."
- "As someone who has done..."
- "Having tried multiple solutions..."
- Role/title drops
- Results/metrics shared

### Skepticism Markers

Language patterns showing distrust:

- "Sounds too good to be true"
- "What's the catch?"
- "I've heard that before"
- "Every tool claims to..."
- "How is this different from..."

## Platform-Specific Collection Tips

### Reddit

- Sort by controversial for emotional intensity
- Sort by top (all time) for validated sentiments
- Read comment threads, not just posts
- Check user post history for context
- Look at crossposted content

### Twitter/X

- Search hashtags + emotional words ("frustrated", "love", "hate")
- Look at quote tweets for stronger opinions
- Check replies to competitor accounts
- Search "[competitor] sucks" or "[competitor] amazing"

### G2/Capterra

- Focus on 2-3 star reviews (balanced opinions)
- Look at "what do you dislike" sections
- Note reviewer company size and role
- Check review date for relevance

### YouTube

- Comments on tutorial videos show knowledge gaps
- Comments on review videos show decision criteria
- Sort by "newest first" for recent sentiments
- Look at like counts on comments for validation

### LinkedIn

- Search posts by role + pain keywords
- Check comments on industry thought leader posts
- Look at poll results and discussions
- Job postings reveal organizational pain

### Habitat Discovery During VoC Collection

While collecting VoC quotes, you're already visiting the places your audience lives. Use this opportunity to identify high-value habitats for the Audience Habitat Map.

**Signals of a high-value habitat:**
- **Concentrated audience activity**: Multiple members of your target persona are active in the same community, not just scattered individuals
- **Recurring discussions**: The same pain points and topics surface repeatedly, indicating the community revolves around relevant themes
- **Community cross-references**: Members mention or link to other communities ("you should also check out [X group]"), revealing habitat networks
- **High engagement on relevant topics**: Posts about your problem space get disproportionate likes, comments, or replies compared to off-topic posts

**What to record when you spot a habitat:**

| Field | What to capture |
|-------|-----------------|
| **Channel Native** | The platform (Twitter/X, Telegram, Discord, Reddit, etc.) |
| **Channel Type** | The kind of community (alpha group, trading community, subreddit, protocol Discord) |
| **Channel Name** | The specific community name (@handle, r/name, group name) |
| **Density** | How concentrated your target audience is here (H/M/L) |
| **Engagement Type** | How your persona participates: Lurker, Engager, Creator, Subscriber, or Searcher |
| **Content consumed** | What type of content gets the most traction with your target audience |

**Tip:** When you find a quote from a high-value habitat, tag it with the habitat name. This creates a direct link between VoC evidence and channel decisions during IMC planning.

## Analysis & Synthesis

### Pattern Recognition Process

1. **Cluster similar quotes** by theme
2. **Count frequency** of each theme
3. **Rate intensity** of emotion expressed
4. **Identify outliers** that reveal hidden insights
5. **Map to product/feature** implications

### Quote Validation Criteria

Before including a quote, verify:

- [ ] Quote is from within last 12 months (unless showing trend)
- [ ] Context is understood and documented
- [ ] Quote is from target audience (not competitor employee, etc.)
- [ ] Emotional tone is accurately assessed
- [ ] Relevance to research goals is clear

## Research Lenses: Psychology Models for Deeper Analysis

Use these models as lenses when analyzing VoC data — they reveal motivations the audience won't state directly.

### Mimetic Desire
People want things because other people want them. In VoC analysis, look for: references to what peers/competitors are doing, "everyone is switching to X," FOMO language, status comparisons. When you see mimetic desire, the audience's stated reason ("it has better features") may mask the real driver ("everyone in my industry uses it and I feel behind").

### Status-Quo Bias
The preference for the current state of affairs, even when alternatives are objectively better. In VoC analysis, look for: "we've always done it this way," rationalizations for current tools despite complaints, fear of migration/switching costs, "the devil you know." Overcoming status-quo bias requires making the cost of NOT switching more vivid than the cost of switching.

### Hyperbolic Discounting
People disproportionately prefer immediate rewards over future rewards. In VoC analysis, look for: impatience with long-term ROI arguments, desire for instant results, "I need this yesterday" language, dismissal of tools that require setup time before delivering value. Messaging implication: lead with what they get TODAY, not what they'll get in 6 months.
