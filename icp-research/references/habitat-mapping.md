# Habitat Mapping

Detailed guidance on discovering and documenting where your target audience naturally congregates online.

---

## What Is a Habitat Map?

A habitat map documents where your audience spends time, what they do there, and how they engage. It feeds directly into IMC channel planning — instead of guessing which channels to use, you choose channels based on where your audience already is.

---

## Habitat Discovery Process

While gathering intelligence in Phase 2 of ICP research, document where each audience segment naturally congregates.

### What to Record

For each platform where you find concentrated audience activity:

| Channel Native | Channel Type | Channel Name | Density | Engagement Type | Habitat Role |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| [Platform] | [Kind of community] | [Specific community] | [H/M/L] | [Lurker/Engager/Creator/Subscriber/Searcher] | [Discovery/Trust/Conversion] |

### Field Definitions

**Density** = How concentrated your target audience is in this habitat:
- **High (H)**: >50% of active members match your ICP
- **Medium (M)**: 20-50% match
- **Low (L)**: <20% match but still present

**Engagement Type** = How they participate:
- **Lurker**: Consumes but rarely engages publicly
- **Engager**: Likes, comments, shares regularly
- **Creator**: Posts own content, active in discussions
- **Subscriber**: Prefers push content (email, notifications)
- **Searcher**: Uses search when problems arise (SEO opportunity)

---

## What to Track During Research

- Note which habitats surface repeatedly across research phases
- Flag communities where your audience clusters densely (H density)
- Record the type of content consumed in each habitat
- Distinguish **discovery habitats** (where they find new things) from **trust habitats** (where they validate decisions) from **conversion habitats** (where they take action)

---

## Habitat Roles

Each habitat plays a role in the audience's decision journey:

| Role | What Happens Here | Example |
|------|-------------------|---------|
| **Discovery** | First encounter with new ideas, products, trends | Twitter/X feed, TikTok For You page, YouTube recommendations |
| **Trust** | Validates claims, seeks peer opinions, does due diligence | Reddit threads, Discord communities, review sites |
| **Conversion** | Takes the target action (sign up, buy, deposit) | Product website, email, direct sales channel |

A single habitat can serve multiple roles for different personas.

## Cross-Persona Habitat Analysis

After mapping habitats for each persona, identify:

1. **Shared habitats** — Where multiple personas overlap. These are high-efficiency channels for broad messaging.
2. **Persona-exclusive habitats** — Where only one persona congregates. Use for targeted, persona-specific messaging.
3. **High-density clusters** — Habitats with H density across multiple personas. Prioritize these for channel investment.

| Habitat | Persona A | Persona B | Persona C | Priority |
|---------|-----------|-----------|-----------|----------|
| [Channel Name] | H | H | M | Highest (shared, high density) |
| [Channel Name] | H | - | - | Persona A only |
| [Channel Name] | - | M | H | Target Personas B & C |
